Innovations are harbingers of future living conditions.

Innovation is a driving force behind progress and change in society. It is the process of creating new ideas or methods, or improving upon existing ones, in order to solve problems or meet new needs. Innovation can take many forms, from technological advancements to social and cultural shifts, and it can have a profound impact on how we live, work, and interact with one another. One of the key benefits of innovation is its ability to drive economic growth. When individuals or organizations come up with new products or services, they create new opportunities for employment and wealth creation. Innovation can also lead to more efficient and effective processes, resulting in cost savings and increased productivity. Innovation is also a key factor in the development of new technologies, which can have far-reaching effects on society. The invention of the steam engine, for example, revolutionized transportation and manufacturing, and led to the Industrial Revolution. Similarly, the development of the internet has had a profound impact on how we communicate, access information, and conduct business. However, innovation is not without its challenges. It can disrupt existing industries and lead to job losses as new technologies and processes replace old ones. It can also create ethical dilemmas, as we grapple with the potential consequences of new technologies and innovations. Innovation is often driven by individuals or organizations that are willing to take risks and think outside the box. It requires creativity, curiosity, and the ability to embrace change. Governments and institutions also play a role in fostering innovation by investing in research and development, supporting entrepreneurship, and creating an environment that encourages risk-taking and experimentation.

A Prototype for an insurance Company

As an insurance company, it's important to have a website that is easy to use, informative, and trustworthy. One way to ensure that your website meets these criteria is by creating a prototype. A prototype is a rough draft of your website that allows you to test and iterate on its design and functionality. It's a valuable tool for any website development project, and it can be especially useful for an insurance company. As part of my internship at Zollhof, my teammate and I worked on a digital solution to help people decide between private or public health insurance for the insurance company HUK Coburg. To do this, we used the different innovation methods like "Design Thinking" and "Lean Startup". Using qualitative interviews and workshops such as UI design, rapid prototyping and much more, we were able to develop a prototype that covered the biggest pain points of potential customers. You can click through the Click Dummy below. Overall, creating a prototype is a crucial step in the process of developing a website for an insurance company. It allows you to test and refine your ideas, communicate your vision to stakeholders, and get feedback from real users. By taking the time to create a prototype, you can ensure that your website is effective, user-friendly, and trustworthy.

Business Canvas for electric Charging

In the summer semester 2017, the Chair of Operational Sustainability Management organized for the first time a seminar for Bachelor students on the topic of energy transition and more sustainable design of mobility, especially electromobility. Our group, called Charge Green, looked at the topic of charging apps for electric cars and found that while there are many apps in this field, no app combines all the functions desired by users. This is where our concept comes in: an app that, among other things, offers a payment option and provides charging stations in the vicinity, including navigation. In mid-July 2017, we were finally able to present our result to an audience and a jury of industry representatives at an evening event at JOSEPHS in Nuremberg. The jury consisted of Christine Wimschneider, research associate at the Chair of Technology Management at FAU, Dr. Patrick Fleischer, Head of Strategic Corporate Development at N-ERGIE, and André Hückstädt, Head of Project Finance at UmweltBank AG. After the project presentations, the jury selected what they considered to be the best project. We were delighted to take first place. This seminar inspired me to take a test drive in a Tesla. I am excited to see what progress we will make in the field of mobility and electric mobility in particular. Below you can take a look at our Business Model Canvas for the project.
